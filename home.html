Any person or human being who is in great poverty is considered to be in poverty. A person is in this circumstance when he or she begins to lack necessities for living, such as a roof over their heads, food, clothing, and medications. Overpopulation, deadly and contagious diseases, natural disasters, low agricultural yields, unemployment, casteism, illiteracy, gender inequality, environmental issues, changing economic trends in the nation, untouchability, and little or limited access to people's rights are all contributing factors to poverty. It is necessary to address issues like political violence, sponsored crime, corruption, a lack of support, passivity, outdated social norms, etc.The issue is that despite the fact that attempts are being made to eradicate poverty around the globe, it has not yet been declared to be a thing of the past.
